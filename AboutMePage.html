Professional Work!

In 7th and 8th grade I was employed at a small virtual reality software startup called Reality Garage. I made one of those VR museum exhibits and competed in the NIST CHARIoT challenge on a few-person team! We won an award for "Most Creative Method of Interation With AR Prototype".

In 11th grade I worked at NIST in the PML lab in Boulder, making a remote-analog-voltage-driver-for-high-speed-polarization-switches (crazy). That was for a quantum entanglement test. In 12th grade, I switched teams and continued to work at NIST on quantum-optical-to-microwave-transduction-for-superconducting-quantum-computers! On man these are tongue twisters.

I am currently working at NREL in Golden, CO, implementing a system to model energy grid loads for hydrogen cell fueling for medium/heavy duty vehicles. I'm adding this feature to an existing modeling software called EVI-Ensite.
